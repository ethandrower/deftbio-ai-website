What's a site-study?

One site participating in one study. It's the unit that carries the compliance work — training, signatures, and the documents an inspector asks for — so it's the one thing we meter. People are always unlimited.

An additional study at a site you already run bills at ~60%, so shared sites stay affordable as your portfolio grows.

How billing works

Predictable by design

You always know your number, because the meter is simple and the rules don't move.

Created = counted

The meter starts when you create a site-study record in Deft — the moment it starts carrying compliance value. Nothing to self-report.

Locked for the term — no proration

Your cost is fixed the day you create the record. If a site closes or withdraws mid-year, the number doesn't change — no surprise true-ups in either direction.

Draft sites don't count

Planning a study? Sites stay in a draft state — free — until you finalize them. You're only billed for site-studies you actually commit to running.

Pricing FAQ

The fine print, in plain English

Do more users cost more?

Never. Users, trainees, and 21 CFR Part 11 signatures are unlimited on every plan. You're only ever billed per site-study.

What happens if a site closes mid-year?

Nothing changes on your bill. A site-study locks for the term the day you create it, so your cost is predictable — there's no mid-term proration in either direction.

I'm still planning a study. Am I billed for sites I haven't started?

No. Sites stay in a free draft state until you finalize them. The meter only starts when you commit a site-study to run.

What if two studies share the same sites?

The first study at a site is billed at the full rate; each additional study at that same site is ~60%. Shared sites stay affordable as you add protocols.

Can I move up a plan as I grow?

Yes — add scope any time at posted rates ($1,200 for a new site, $700 for an additional study at an existing site), and step up to the next band whenever it's the better deal.
